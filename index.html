Where to Start

MCAT Study Strategy

The MCAT tests your ability to integrate knowledge across subjects and reason through novel problems — not just recall facts. Build your foundations first, then use the MCAT-specific modules to practice applying what you know at exam difficulty.

Recommended Approach

1

Shore up your content foundations

Use the Biology, Chemistry, and Physics review guides below to identify and fill gaps. Attempting MCAT-style questions without solid content knowledge is frustrating and unproductive.

2

Start with the Adaptive Practice Engine

Begin at difficulty level 1 and work upward. The Adaptive Practice Engine covers biology, biochemistry, chemistry, genetics, and organic chemistry — and shows you exactly where your weak spots are.

3

Learn systems thinking through Disease Pathways

The MCAT frequently crosses organ systems in single questions. Disease Pathways teaches you to trace physiological connections through eight memorable genetic diseases — this is how you train yourself to think the way the exam requires.

4

Build CARS skills early and consistently

CARS is the section most students underestimate and underwork. Start with CARS Cognitive Drills to build specific reading skills, then move to full Practice Passages for timed work. A little CARS every day beats cramming.

5

Push to exam difficulty with Medium-Hard questions

Once comfortable at levels 1–3, switch to the Medium-Hard module for concentrated practice at levels 3–5. This is where you close the gap between knowing content and performing under pressure.

Study Paths by Timeline

3+ Months Out

You have time. Build content systematically before drilling.

Work through Biology & Chemistry review guides
Adaptive Practice at low difficulty
CARS Cognitive Drills (daily)
Disease Pathways for systems thinking
Systems Learning for deep connections
Ramp up to Medium-Hard questions

4 to 8 Weeks Out

Focus on high-yield practice and weakest areas.

Adaptive Practice at difficulty 3+ daily
CARS Practice Passages (timed)
Medium-Hard questions daily
Psychology & Sociology review
Subject guides for remaining weak spots

Final 2 Weeks

Review and integrate — do not cram new material.

Disease Pathways for systems review
CARS Passages to stay sharp
Adaptive Quiz Tool for mixed review
Psychology & Sociology flashcards
