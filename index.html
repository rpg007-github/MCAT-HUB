Where to Start

MCAT Study Strategy

The MCAT tests your ability to integrate knowledge across subjects and reason through novel problems. These tools are designed around that reality. Build your foundations first, then use the MCAT-specific modules to practice applying what you know.

Recommended Approach

1

Shore up your foundations

Use the Biology, Chemistry, and Physics review guides to fill any gaps in your content knowledge. These are the reference material you need before you can tackle MCAT-style questions effectively.

2

Start with the Adaptive Practice Engine

Begin at difficulty level 1 and work your way up. The adaptive engine covers biology, biochemistry, chemistry, genetics, and organic chemistry. Use it to identify your weak spots.

3

Learn systems thinking with Disease Pathways

The MCAT loves to ask questions that cross organ systems. The Disease Pathways module teaches you to trace physiological connections through eight memorable genetic diseases. This is how you train yourself to think the way the exam requires.

4

Build CARS skills early and often

CARS is the section most students underestimate. Start the Cognitive Drills to build the specific reading skills CARS demands, then move to the full Practice Passages for timed, exam-style practice. Do a little CARS work every day.

5

Level up with Medium-Hard questions

Once you are comfortable at difficulty levels 1 through 3, switch to the Medium-Hard module for focused practice at levels 3 through 5. This is where you push into exam-level difficulty.

Study Paths

Prep Paths by Timeline

3+ Months Out

You have time. Build foundations systematically before drilling.

Work through Biology and Chemistry review guides
Start Adaptive Practice at low difficulty
Begin CARS Cognitive Drills (daily)
Disease Pathways for systems integration
Systems Learning Approach for deep connections
Ramp up to Medium-Hard questions

4 to 8 Weeks Out

Focus on high-yield practice and your weakest areas.

Adaptive Practice at difficulty 3+ daily
CARS Practice Passages (timed, exam conditions)
Medium-Hard questions for targeted drilling
Psychology and Sociology review
Subject guides for any remaining weak spots

Final 2 Weeks

Review, do not cram. Focus on high-yield integration.

Disease Pathways for systems review
CARS passages to stay sharp
Adaptive Quiz Tool for mixed review
Psychology and Sociology flashcards
